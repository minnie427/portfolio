PURPLE ISLAND



PROJECT DESCRIPTION

“Purple Island” is a 3D model of a floating island located somewhere in the mystical universe. The island is adorned with stunning and ethereal plants and flowers, immersing visitors in a mesmerizing sea of various shades of purple. Several areas on the island provide perfect spots to appreciate MONTSENU’s latest clothing lines, while recycled plastic bottles float around, serving as a reminder for us to continually strive for a more sustainable approach in plastic usage. Delicate petals cascade from the island, creating a breathtaking and dreamlike spectacle.


Primary Software: MAYA








Rendering from Maya















USE OF ADVANCED TECHNIQUE


By exploring and implementing these technical techniques in Maya, the project’s visual quality and level of realism can be significantly elevated, providing a more immersive and engaging experience for viewers.






Grass, Plants, Flowers: Stand-ins + MASH

To achieve a diverse range of plants on the island, stand-ins are utilized within a MASH instancing network. This approach enables thousands of selected assets to be distributed across the top plane mesh, which is modeled to resemble a terrain. Stand-ins play a crucial role in optimizing the performance of the instancing network, as they allow Maya to compute and render the scene efficiently. These stand-ins are created by converting the polygons into the ASS (Arnold Scene Source) format, which facilitates faster and lighter istancing calculations.

Branches: Rigging

A tree trunk with straight branches is modeled, and joints are added to each branch, acting as bones for the tree. Rigging is employed to create a hierarchical structure that is bound to each branch, enabling flexible control and manipulation. Utilizing rigging in trees is highly beneficial as it allows for the articulation of branches in a lifelike manner, achieving natural motion and dynamics. This technique enhances the realism of the tree’s movement and brings a sense of organic motion to the overall scene.







Cloth: Ncloth, Wind effect

Maya’s Ncloth feature was utilized to simulate the natural behavior of cloth, specifically a cloth plane swaying in the wind. This powerful tool enables the creation of realistic cloth simulations by defining physical properties such as elasticity, friction, and gravity. Applying Ncloth to a cloth plane allows for the achievement of lifelike draping, folding, and movement, resulting in visually convincing cloth dynamics.
